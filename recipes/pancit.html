Pancit

Description

Pancit is a Filipino noodle dish with Chinese origins. The name "pancit" comes from the Hokkien Chinese term "pian e sit," meaning "something conveniently cooked". It's a staple in Filipino cuisine, enjoyed for its versatility, flavor, and ease of preparation.

Ingredients

  • 8-16oz thin rice noodles
  • 1/4 head of cabbage
  • 1/2 package shredded carrots
  • 25 pods of snow peas
  • 2 chicken breasts, sliced thin
  • 4 Kam Yen Jan Chinese style sausages (optional)
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 spoons soy sauce
  • 1 spoon fish sauce
  • 1-2 spoons oyster sauce
  • 1 cup water

Steps

  1. Soften noodles by soaking them in water for ~30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Saute onions, garlic, and chicken with salt and pepper to taste. Set to the side.
  3. Add water, soy sauce, fish sauce, and oyster sauce into pan.
  4. In a separate pan, saute carrots, cabbage, and snow peas.
  5. Add everything to the water mixture and cook down until desired texture.
